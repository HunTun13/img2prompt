Write the edit like a clear creative brief

Nano Banana works well with direct natural language. The useful detail is not extra adjectives—it is a clear separation between the change and the parts that stay fixed.

Name the requested change

Open with an action such as replace, remove, restyle, relight, or extend instead of a loose tag list.

Protect the subject

Explicitly preserve identity, facial features, product geometry, pose, or other defining details when consistency matters.

Set the boundaries

Say what must not change: framing, camera angle, background elements, lighting direction, typography, or proportions.

A good edit instruction has four parts: current subject and scene, requested change, desired visual treatment, and unchanged constraints.

Nano Banana prompt examples

Each prompt makes one edit understandable while protecting the visual features that should remain consistent.

Change a portrait background

Goal: move an indoor portrait outdoors without changing the person.

Edit this portrait by replacing the plain studio wall with a softly blurred autumn park at golden hour. Preserve the subject's identity, facial features, expression, hairstyle, charcoal jacket, pose, and exact crop. Match the new warm background light to the existing direction of light on the face. Do not change body proportions or add accessories.

Create a product colorway

Goal: recolor a sneaker while keeping its design and presentation fixed.

Change only the sneaker's blue fabric panels to deep forest green. Keep the sole, stitching, laces, logo placement, materials, product shape, camera angle, white background, and soft studio shadow exactly the same. Preserve all fine construction details and realistic fabric texture.

Restyle an interior

Goal: update the furnishings without redesigning the architecture.

Restyle this living room with quiet Japanese-Scandinavian furnishings: a low oak sofa frame, off-white linen upholstery, one dark ceramic side table, and a neutral wool rug. Preserve the room dimensions, windows, floor, ceiling beams, camera position, perspective, and daylight direction. Remove visual clutter but do not alter the architecture.

A reusable edit-instruction pattern

  • Action: “Replace the background…” or “Change only the jacket color…”
  • Target and treatment: describe exactly which region or object changes and what the finished result should look like.
  • Constraints: finish with what must not change, including identity, composition, proportions, light direction, and important design details.

Nano Banana image-to-prompt FAQ

Should I use keywords or sentences?

Use concise, complete sentences. Direct instructions make the edit and its boundaries easier to understand than a large pile of style tags.

How do I preserve a face?

Ask to preserve the subject's identity, facial structure, expression, hairstyle, and pose, then limit the requested change to a clearly named area.

Can the tool identify a real person?

No. Img2Prompt describes visible characteristics and should not be used to guess or confirm a person's identity.
